🧮 What is the Analytic Hierarchy Process?

The Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP) is a structured decision-making framework developed by mathematician Thomas Saaty in the 1970s. It's used everywhere from military procurement to urban planning — and now, the Premier League relegation battle.

AHP works by breaking a complex decision down into smaller, manageable pairwise comparisons. Instead of asking "what do you prefer overall?" — which is hard to answer — it asks a series of focused questions like "for this specific match, which outcome matters more to you, and by how much?"

1
Define the Goal

Determine your most preferred EPL season outcome.

2
Identify Criteria

Each remaining Premier League fixture is a "criterion" contributing to the outcome.

3
Rate Your Preferences

For each match, use the Saaty scale (1–9) to rate how strongly you prefer one outcome over the other.

4
Compute Priority Weights

AHP builds a pairwise matrix and derives importance weights using the eigenvector method.

5
Calculate OVP

The Overall Value Priority (OVP) aggregates your weighted preferences to reveal your true preferred outcome.

6
Check Consistency

The Consistency Ratio (CR) validates your preferences. CR < 0.1 indicates a reliable result.

📏 The Saaty Scale

When comparing two criteria, the Saaty scale provides a standardised way to express relative importance:

Intensity Definition
1 Equal importance — no preference between outcomes
3 Moderate preference — one outcome is slightly more important
5 Strong preference — one outcome is clearly more important
7 Very strong preference — one outcome is strongly favoured
9 Extreme preference — one outcome is overwhelmingly more important
